During my freshman year at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa I took a general education course in the geography department. The professor mentioned having meteorological station running up the slopes of Mount Kaʻala. This peaked my interest because I had worked on this mountain the previous summer as a Kupu Americore conservation team member. I approached the professor and offered my help to automate data analysis proccesies using the R programming language. 

He agreed and later that year we submitted a proster presentation to the American Geophysical Fall Meeting (virtual 2020) https://agu2020fallmeeting-agu.ipostersessions.com/Default.aspx?s=76-A1-DE-D7-1F-32-28-4A-73-E8-86-7C-F9-C4-92-52.

During this project I used R to work with comma-delimited data and create slope environmental lapse rates for Temperature and relative humidity on the windward slopes of the mountain.
